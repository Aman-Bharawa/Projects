PLACES TO VISIT

Discribtion about a few must visit places in Ujjain

Triveni Museum

Triveni Archaeological Museum is being established by Directorate of Archaeology, Archives and Museums M.P. Government on the occasion of Simhasth 2016. It comprises of 3 galleries named Shaiv, Vaishnava and Shakt. The Triveni concepts is based on the composition of three cult particular Shaivayan (Shiv), Krishnayan(Vasudev Krishna) and Durgayan (goddess Durga). The stone sculpture, coins, miniature painting and other material have been displayed in these gallery.

Ram Ghat

Ram Ghat is of immense religious significance to Hindus as it is one of the four locations where the Kumbh Mela takes place every 12 years. It is believed that Lord Vishnu dribbled some drops of Amrit at Ram Ghat. It is considered to be one of the oldest bathing Ghats in connection with the Kumbh celebrations.

Eco Park

Eco Park in Ujjain is a serene and eco-friendly haven nestled in the heart of the historic city of Ujjain, India. This lush urban oasis spans across acres of meticulously landscaped greenery, making it a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The park is a testament to Ujjain's commitment to preserving its natural beauty and promoting environmental awareness. Visitors to Eco Park can relish the sights and sounds of nature while strolling through well-maintained gardens, tranquil ponds, and shaded pathways. It's not only a place of leisure but also a center for environmental education and conservation, with informative exhibits and programs that promote sustainable living and biodiversity. Eco Park in Ujjain offers a tranquil retreat for locals and tourists alike, providing a glimpse into the harmonious coexistence of nature and urban life. It stands as a symbol of Ujjain's dedication to protecting the environment and nurturing green spaces within the city.

Mahakaal Lok

On Tuesday, October 11, Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Mahakal Lok, the 920-metre-long mega corridor. The corridor has around 200 statues and it is spread along the Rudra Sagar Lake. A total of eight forms of Lord Shiva are available for Mahakal Lok Darshana. The corridor also showcases murals of the stories, which are mentioned in the Shiva Purana. The corridor has a total of 108 pillars and two grand entrance gates – Nandi Gate and Pinaki Gate. This 920-metre long corridor leads to the entrance of the famous Mahakaleshwar Temple and it also offers a mesmerising panoramic view along the way. The murals depict the concept of Mahakal Forest from the epic of Meghdoot, written by Mahakavi Kalidas.It is the third ‘jyotirlinga’ site to see a major upliftment after Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi and Kedarnath Shrine in Uttarakhand. However, Ujjain’s Mahakal Lok corridor is four times the size of Vishwanath Temple corridor in Varanasi.After this upliftment the complex has now become ten times bigger in size. Previously it was spread in only two hectares, but now the entire complex spreads over 20 hectares. Ujjain Mahakal Lok Corridor is constructed near Ujjain Mahakaleshwar Temple, which houses the Swayambhu form of Lord Shiva.Mahakaleshwar Temple is one of the 12 sacred jyotirlingas in the world and it is one of the must-visit places in Ujjain.A sum of Rs. 856 crores has been spent on developing the first phase of the Ujjain Mahakal Lok Temple Corridor. Ujjain Mahakal Lok Religious Tour will now include a tour of the temple as well as this newly-opened mega corridor.

Vishnu Sagar

Vishnu Sagar is a picturesque and revered water body located in the ancient city of Ujjain, India. This tranquil lake holds significant religious and cultural importance, making it a prominent destination for pilgrims and tourists alike. Legend has it that Vishnu Sagar is connected to the Hindu god Vishnu, and taking a dip in its holy waters is believed to cleanse one's soul. The lake is surrounded by ghats and temples, which are frequently visited by devotees who come to offer prayers and perform rituals. The serene ambiance of Vishnu Sagar, coupled with its historical significance, makes it a peaceful retreat for those seeking spiritual solace. Its tranquil waters mirror the temples and ghats that line its shores, creating a mesmerizing and serene atmosphere. Vishnu Sagar not only serves as a religious hub but also a place of tranquility and reflection, where visitors can experience the rich spiritual heritage of Ujjain while enjoying the scenic beauty of this sacred lake.

Gopal Mandir

Gopal Mandir is a prominent area in Ujjain, known for its bustling and vibrant market. This area is a commercial hub that caters to both locals and tourists. The market around Gopal Mandir offers a wide range of goods, making it a one-stop destination for shopping. Visitors can find an array of items, including clothing, jewelry, handicrafts, religious artifacts, and traditional Indian garments. The market is particularly known for its jewelry shops, offering intricate and exquisite pieces, often incorporating traditional Indian designs. Additionally, the area has numerous street food stalls and local eateries, allowing visitors to savor delicious local cuisine and snacks. The ambiance is lively, and the market is often crowded, especially during festivals and religious events, making it a vibrant and culturally rich part of Ujjain. The Gopal Mandir market encapsulates the essence of the city, blending tradition, commerce, and spirituality.

Tower Chowk

Tower Chowk, located in the heart of Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh, is a place that embodies the city's rich history, culture, and religious significance. This bustling intersection is dominated by the iconic Ksheer Sagar Tower, more commonly known as the Clock Tower, which stands as a symbolic and practical reference point for both locals and visitors. Tower Chowk holds immense importance in the daily life and cultural heritage of Ujjain.

  • Religious Significance:

    Tower Chowk is intimately linked with Ujjain's spiritual and religious identity. It is a gateway to some of the city's most revered temples and ghats. During the Kumbh Mela, an important Hindu pilgrimage that Ujjain hosts every 12 years, Tower Chowk becomes a focal point. Pilgrims gather here before proceeding to the holy ghats along the banks of the Shipra River, making it a hub for religious activities.

  • Historical Landmark:

    The Clock Tower, built during the Holkar dynasty's rule in the 18th century, stands as a testament to the city's rich historical past. It has been an essential landmark for generations, guiding travelers through the winding streets of Ujjain.

  • Cultural Hub:

    The market surrounding Tower Chowk is a vibrant and bustling space. It offers a diverse range of goods, from clothing and accessories to handicrafts and religious items. The market is a blend of tradition and commerce, representing the cultural ethos of Ujjain. Local street food vendors line the streets, offering delicious regional snacks and specialties that allow visitors to experience the city's culinary heritage.

  • Meeting Point:

    Tower Chowk is not only a geographical meeting point but also a social and cultural one. Locals often use it as a reference to plan their meetings, and tourists find it a helpful starting point for exploring the city.

Kothi Road

Kothi Road in Ujjain is a notable thoroughfare that holds both historical significance and modern relevance in this ancient city of Madhya Pradesh, India. Stretching through the heart of Ujjain, Kothi Road serves as a lifeline connecting various parts of the city and is a witness to its evolving character.

  • Historical Legacy:

    Kothi Road is not just any road; it's a journey through time. Lined with grand havelis and old mansions, this road is a testament to the city's architectural heritage. Some of these havelis date back to the Maratha and Holkar era, showcasing intricate design and craftsmanship.

  • Commercial Hub:

    Today, Kothi Road is a bustling commercial center in Ujjain. It hosts numerous shops, boutiques, and markets that cater to the various needs of residents and tourists alike. From clothing stores to jewelry shops and local eateries, it's a one-stop destination for shopping and experiencing local flavors.

  • Cultural Diversity:

    The road encapsulates the cultural diversity of Ujjain. It's not uncommon to find people from different backgrounds strolling along this road, adding to its vibrant atmosphere.

  • Accessibility:

    Due to its central location, Kothi Road is easily accessible by both residents and tourists. It serves as a starting point for exploring the city's many attractions.
In essence, Kothi Road is more than just a road; it's a microcosm of Ujjain's rich history, cultural diversity, and modern dynamics. It seamlessly blends the city's past and present, making it an essential part of the Ujjain experience.
